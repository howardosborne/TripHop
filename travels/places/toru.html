Toruń

As one of the oldest cities in Poland, Toruń has along legacy in the city’s architecture, ranging from Brick Gothic to Mannerist and Baroque. The medieval part of the city has been designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site. In 2007 the Old Town of Toruń was added to the list of Seven Wonders of Poland. The town is also famous for gingerbread and It as the birthplace of Copernicus.
