Austria

How to get to Austria flight-free

Being in the centre of Europe makes it one of the most accessible places to get to by train or coach.

Travelling to Vienna by train

There are so many great ways to end up in Vienna. The typical routes from London involve going down the high speed lines shown here.

There are fantastic places to stop en-route like:

  • Aachen, a spa resort steeped in the history of the Holy Roman Empire
  • Cologne, evokitively set on the Rhine
  • Frankfurt, modern day centre of European finance but also the home of Goethe
  • Nuremberg, with its castle, Durer and tasty sausages
  • Regensburg, pretty town with the dramatic Wallhalla nearby

Other popular routes

Here are some popular train routes to Austria

  • Brussels to Vienna
  • Paris to Vienna
  • Berlin to Vienna
  • Budapest to Vienna

Getting to Austria by coach

Travelling from Northern Europe to Vienna by coach in one go is… character building, taking 15 hours from Trier or 20 hours from Antwerp.

However, before rulling out coach travel all together, it’s worth remembering that while coaches may (usually) be slower than trains but they have some advantages…

  1. they are (usually) cheaper
  2. they go over hills, sometimes taking you through beautiful scenery (my favourite route in to Spain is by coach)
  3. there are more roads than rails so you can reach the parts that trains can’t reach
  4. they can be incredibly flexible - railway infrastructure is complex…

You can break the journey up, by hopping off at some fantastic places on the way like: Passau and Regensburg.

Belgium

How to get to Belgium flight-free from UK

Being at the political heart of Europe, Belgium, and in particular Brussels, is one of the most accessible places to get to by train or coach.

How to get to Belgium by rail

Regular Eurostar/Thalys trains run from London to Brussels taking around 2 hours. However, Eurostar can be expensive, particularly at short notice.

How to get to Belgium by coach

It takes 6 hours 40 mins by coach from London to Brussels but there are shorter journeys which might make more sense depending on your circumstances.

It takes just over three hours from Dover to Bruges and the next stop, Ghent is half an hour down the road.

Getting to Hungary by coach

Travelling from Northern Europe to Budapest by coach in one go is character building…

However, before rulling out coach travel all together, it’s worth remembering that while coaches may (usually) be slower than trains but they have some advantages…

  1. they are (usually) cheaper
  2. they go over hills, sometimes taking you through beautiful scenery (my favourite route in to Spain is by coach)
  3. there are more roads than rails so you can reach the parts that trains can’t reach
  4. they can be incredibly flexible - railway infrastructure is complex…

You can break the journey up, by hopping off at some fantastic places on the way like: Passau, Regensburg and Vienna.
